Module 1

AI Fundamentals

A practical baseline for understanding what modern AI is, what it is not, and how to talk about it clearly in a professional setting.

What you’ll be able to do
  • Explain AI in one sentence
  • Describe core capabilities and limitations
  • Differentiate AI vs automation (and RPA)
  • Identify safe, high-value use cases

Definition + Non-Definition

Simple language people can repeat accurately.

What AI is

AI refers to systems capable of perception, reasoning, decision-making, and learning from data.

Perception Reasoning Decision-making Learning
Say it like this
“AI is software that learns patterns from data to help interpret, predict, or generate outputs.”

What AI is not

  • Not sentient (no consciousness or intent)
  • Not magic (outputs can be wrong)
  • Not a replacement for professional judgment
Important
AI can accelerate work, but it does not remove accountability. Humans remain responsible for decisions and deliverables.

Why it’s different
GenAI produces fluent output quickly — you validate it like you would a first draft from a junior staff member.

Types of AI

Be precise about what exists today vs what is theoretical.

Narrow AI (today)

Task-specific systems (chatbots, classification, anomaly detection, recommendations).

General AI (theoretical)

Human-level intelligence across a broad range of tasks.

Super AI (hypothetical)

Intelligence beyond humans.

AI vs Automation

This is where most confusion happens — so we make it explicit.

Dimension Automation AI
Logic Rule-based (“if X then Y”) Data-driven (learned patterns)
Best for Repetitive, predictable steps Ambiguity, variability, language, judgment-support
Change handling Breaks when inputs change Can adapt if trained/updated
Risk Incorrect rules = consistent errors Incorrect output = needs validation
Where RPA fits
RPA is automation. It can be enhanced by AI (e.g., reading emails, classifying documents) to handle more complex work.

Workplace Reality

A grounded view: opportunities + constraints.

Opportunities

  • Speed: faster drafts + analysis
  • Consistency: structured outputs
  • New services: insights and automation accelerators

Challenges

  • Data quality + confidentiality
  • Change management
  • Governance + review standards
Professional standard
Treat AI output as a draft. Validate facts, citations, calculations, and client-specific details before use.

Key Takeaways

  1. AI is a tool — not a replacement for humans.
  2. AI literacy is now baseline professional capability.
  3. Responsible use (privacy, accuracy, governance) is critical.

Glossary
Quick definitions for live discussion
NLP
Natural Language Processing — understanding and generating human language.
Machine Learning
Learning patterns from examples to make predictions or decisions.
Generative AI
AI that creates new content (text, images, code) from learned patterns.
Automation
Rule-based execution of steps; consistent, predictable, not “learning.”
RPA
Robotic Process Automation — automation of repetitive tasks; can be enhanced by AI.
