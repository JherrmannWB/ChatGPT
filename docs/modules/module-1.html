AI at a glance

AI refers to computer systems that perform tasks that normally require human intelligence: understanding language, recognizing patterns, learning from experience, making predictions, and generating content. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

What AI is

  • Understanding language (NLP)
  • Recognizing patterns in data
  • Learning from examples (machine learning)
  • Predicting outcomes
  • Generating new content (GenAI)

What AI is not

  • Not sentient
  • Not “magic” or fully autonomous
  • Not a replacement for human judgment

Best results come from human + AI collaboration. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Why AI matters today
  • AI is transforming industries (including public accounting) and everyday experiences.
  • AI literacy helps professionals stay competitive, compliant, and innovative.

Examples listed in Module 1 include voice assistants, recommendations, and automated support. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Core AI capabilities

Four “buckets” from the deck. Click each to expand. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

1) Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Understanding and generating human language (text, sometimes speech).

summarization Q&A drafting
2) Pattern Recognition

Finding trends, anomalies, similarities, and signals in data.

anomaly detection classification clustering
3) Machine Learning & Prediction

Learning from past data to predict likely future outcomes.

forecasting risk scoring recommendations
4) Generative AI

Creating new content (text, images, code) based on learned patterns.

emails meeting notes draft deliverables

Types of AI

Narrow AI exists today; General and Super AI are theoretical/hypothetical. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Narrow AI (today)

Designed for specific tasks (chatbots, image recognition, fraud detection).

General AI (theoretical)

Would match human intelligence across a wide range of tasks.

Super AI (hypothetical)

Would surpass human intelligence.

AI vs Automation

Automation follows rules; AI learns/adapts from data. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Automation

  • Rule-based
  • Consistent, predictable steps
  • Great for repetitive processes

AI

  • Data-driven
  • Can learn patterns
  • Can handle variability and ambiguity
